Each time you see a targeted ad, your personal data has just been exposed to thousands of advertisers & data brokers through a process called "real-time bidding" (RTB). This lets data brokers harvest & monetize your online activity, which fuels government surveillance. 🧵(1/11)

What's real-time bidding (RTB)? It's the process used to select the targeted ads shown to you on nearly every site and app you visit. Those ads are the winners of milliseconds-long auctions that expose your personal info to thousands of companies a day. (2/11)

The auction starts by sending thousands of potential advertisers your personal data, including your unique advertising ID, location, IP address, and demographic information. While only one advertiser wins the auction, all advertisers can keep your personal data (3/11).

Essentially, anyone posing as an ad buyer can access a stream of sensitive data about billions of individuals using sites or apps with targeted ads. This is a big way that data brokers can get a hold of personal data that they then sell to basically anyone willing to pay. (4/11)

While some data collection methods require web or app developers to install code from a data broker, RTB is facilitated by ad companies that are already plugged into most websites and apps. This allows data brokers to collect data at a staggering scale. (5/11)

Hundreds of billions of RTB bid requests are broadcast every day. As a result, entire businesses have emerged to harvest and sell data from online advertising auctions. (6/11)

The dangers of RTB aren't hypothetical. The FTC recently found that data broker Mobilewalla was collecting personal data from auctions without placing ads. Mobilewalla then sold this data for invasive purposes, including tracking union organizers and people at Black Lives Matter protests. (7/11)

There's also the fact that data brokers have been caught selling this data to government intelligence agencies like the U.S. Defense Department. (8/11)

There are steps you can take to protect your private information. For apps: This blog has instructions to disable your mobile ad ID and check which apps have access to your location info. For sites: Install @privacybadger.org, a free browser extension built by EFF to block online trackers. (9/11)

The best way to prevent online ads from fueling surveillance is to ban online behavioral advertising. This would end the practice of targeting ads based on your online activity. Seeing an ad shouldn't mean surrendering your data to thousands of companies you've never even heard of. (10/11)

It's time to end this type of advertising and the mass surveillance it's enabling. To learn more about real-time bidding and the surveillance threat it poses to all, you can read the full article. (11/11)

Online Behavioral Ads Fuel the Surveillance Industry—Here’s How
